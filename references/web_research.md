# Research for Decisive Information

## The Core Principle

Research should be driven by **what you need to know**, not by a list of searches to execute. Finding 10 articles that say the same thing is 1x insight with 10x confirmation bias.

**The goal: find the one fact that changes your thesis.**

For the full research protocol, see `references/research_for_edge.md`.

---

## Research Workflow

### Phase 1: Identify What You Need to Know

Before searching, answer:

1. What is my key unknown?
2. What specific fact would resolve it?
3. What type of source would have it?

### Phase 2: Search for Variables, Not Tickers

| Bad Search | Good Search |
|-----------|-------------|
| "AAPL stock news" | "iPhone upgrade cycle replacement rate 2025" |
| "TSLA analysis" | "Tesla China market share vs BYD Q1 2026" |
| "NVDA forecast" | "Hyperscaler AI capex plans 2026-2027" |
| "AMZN bull case" | "AWS revenue growth deceleration rate 2026" |

Search for the **decisive variable**, not the company name.

### Phase 3: Primary Sources First

Source quality hierarchy:

1. **Primary**: Earnings transcripts, SEC filings, company IR, court records
2. **Expert**: Industry reports, analyst deep-dives, specialized publications
3. **Quality journalism**: WSJ, FT, Bloomberg, Reuters
4. **Aggregate**: Seeking Alpha, Yahoo Finance
5. **Social/pump**: Twitter, YouTube, Discord

**Always reach for Tier 1-2.** Tier 4-5 gives you what everyone already knows.

### Phase 4: Seek Dissent

After finding supporting evidence, INTENTIONALLY search for the opposing view:

- "[ASSET] bear case" / "[ASSET] short thesis"
- "[ASSET] risks not discussed"
- "[ASSET] regulatory investigation"
- "[ASSET] insider selling"

**The contradicting fact is MORE valuable than the confirming one.**

### Phase 5: Cross-Verify

Never trust one source for a critical fact:
- "Beat by $0.23" → Check the actual earnings release
- "Revenue up 18%" → Organic or includes acquisitions?
- "Analyst raised to $300" → Consensus or outlier?

### Phase 6: Stop When Marginal Research Stops Changing Your Thesis

The stopping test: "Would the next article likely change my recommendation?" If no, stop.

---

## Minimum Research Standards

You MUST:
1. Search for the decisive variable (not just the ticker)
2. Reach at least one primary or expert source
3. Seek at least one opposing perspective
4. Cross-verify at least one critical fact
5. Identify the recent catalyst (what moved the stock recently)
6. Find upcoming catalysts (earnings date, events)

**Skip at your own risk.** This is the difference between shallow and deep research.

---

## Integration with Analysis

Research is not a separate step — it feeds directly into your thesis.

- **Does research support or contradict your initial thesis?**
- **Does it resolve your key unknowns?**
- **Does it reveal new risks or opportunities?**
- **Does it change your conviction level?**

If research changes your thesis, that's the HIGHEST-VALUE outcome. Don't resist it.

**Changing your mind based on evidence is the definition of intelligence.**
