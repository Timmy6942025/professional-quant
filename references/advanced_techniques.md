# Advanced Analytical Techniques

## Market Regime Detection
Determine current market environment to contextualize signals:

### Bull Market Characteristics
- Rising 200-day SMA, price above 200 SMA
- Low VIX (<20), declining volatility
- Sector rotation: cyclicals → growth → defensive
- IPO activity high, momentum strong

### Bear Market Characteristics  
- Falling 200-day SMA, price below 200 SMA
- High VIX (>25), expanding volatility
- Flight to quality: defensive > growth > cyclicals
- Capitulation events, failed rallies

### Transition/Choppy Market
- Mixed signals, whipsaws
- Range-bound price action
- VIX elevated but not extreme
- Best for mean reversion, worst for momentum

## Peer & Sector Analysis
Always compare stock to:
1. **Sector ETF** (e.g., XLK for tech, XLF for financials)
2. **Market ETF** (SPY, QQQ)
3. **Peers** (e.g., for AMZN: compare to MSFT, GOOGL, META)

Relative strength = Stock return / Sector return
- >1.2: Outperforming strongly
- 0.8-1.2: Normal performance
- <0.8: Underperforming

## Multi-Timeframe Analysis
NEVER make decisions on single timeframe:

### Daily (Short-term, 1-30 days)
- RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands most relevant
- Volume confirmation critical
- Momentum strategies work best

### Weekly (Medium-term, 1-6 months)
- SMA 50/200 crossovers
- Sector rotation signals
- Earnings momentum

### Monthly (Long-term, 6+ months)
- Secular trends
- Interest rate environment
- Business cycle position

## Asymmetric Opportunity Detection
Look for situations with:
- **Limited downside, unlimited upside**: Options-like payoffs
- **High Sharpe, low drawdown**: Efficient risk usage
- **Contrarian setups**: Everybody hates it (sentiment extreme)
- **Catalyst-driven**: Event that could rerate stock

## Volatility Analysis
Volatility is not just risk - it's opportunity:

### Expansion vs Contraction
- **Volatility expansion**: Big moves coming (earnings, news)
- **Volatility contraction**: Powder keg (bollinger band squeeze)
- **Historical percentile**: Current vol vs 1-year range

### Volatility Risk Premium
If implied vol (options) > realized vol: Sell volatility
If implied vol < realized vol: Buy volatility (if options available)

## Correlation Analysis
High correlation = diversification doesn't work
- Tech stocks: ~0.7-0.8 correlation
- During crashes: All correlations → 1.0
- Look for idiosyncratic opportunities (low correlation to market)

## The "Insane" Edge
What separates good from insane analysis:

1. **Second-level thinking**: "I know that you know that I know"
2. **Base rate analysis**: How often does this pattern lead to profits?
3. **Feedback loops**: Identify self-reinforcing cycles
4. **Reflexivity**: Price affects fundamentals, which affect price
5. **Narrative vs Reality**: What story is priced in vs what's true?

## Red Flags in Your Own Analysis
- "This time is different" (it never is)
- Ignoring contrary evidence
- Over-relying on one indicator
- Anchoring to purchase price
- Confirmation bias in signal selection
